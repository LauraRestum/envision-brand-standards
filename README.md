# Envision Brand Standards

The operational source of truth for all Envision content, written and AI-generated.

**Version 2.0 | June 2026**

This repository holds the **Envision AI Directive**, the single governing document for how Envision content is written and produced. It supersedes the Envision AI Brand Directive v1.0, the earlier AI Messaging Directive draft, and the legacy brand book. Where any of those conflict with this repository, this repository governs.

---

## Who this is for

This is an operational directive, not a style essay. Anyone should be able to open it cold and produce on-brand Envision content. It is written for three users at once:

- **Envision staff**
- **Outside vendors and partners**
- **AI tools and agents** generating content on Envision's behalf

> **AI tools and agents:** read [Instructions for AI tools and agents](docs/00-how-to-use-this-directive.md#instructions-for-ai-tools-and-agents) first, then follow the order of operations without exception.

---

## The three rules that settle most questions

1. **Capability before mission.** In commercial and government work, prove Envision can do the job first. Mission is the closer, not the opener.
2. **Person before program.** Lead with the person and their agency in mission work. Envision enables. People thrive.
3. **Most conservative rule wins.** When two rules seem to compete, or you are unsure, choose the stricter, more respectful option and flag it for review.

---

## Order of operations

Apply the rules in this order every time. Each layer sits on top of the one before it.

1. **Context.** Commercial, government, mission-program, donor, or internal. Sets the voice. See [Section 2](docs/02-mission-and-credibility-rule.md).
2. **Section 8 or a pillar.** Commercial and government work uses [Section 8](docs/08-commercial-and-government.md); otherwise identify the pillar in [Section 9](docs/09-writing-about-each-part.md).
3. **Audience.** Apply the audience directive in [Section 10](docs/10-audience-directives.md) on top of the pillar.
4. **Asset type.** Apply the asset rules in [Section 11](docs/11-asset-type-rules.md) for the format you are producing. For anything designed or visual, also apply [Section 12](docs/12-visual-and-design-direction.md).
5. **Universal rules.** [Sections 5](docs/05-writing-style-and-terminology.md), [6](docs/06-forbidden-patterns.md), and [7](docs/07-word-swap-reference.md) apply to everything, always. Never optional.

---

## Hard failures (never, in any context)

- **No em dashes** anywhere.
- **Never use the word _impaired_** in any form (including _visually impaired_, _blind or visually impaired_).
- **Never use the acronym _BVI_.**

See [Section 6: Forbidden patterns](docs/06-forbidden-patterns.md) for the full list.

## Governance

Maintained by **Envision Marketing**. Contact **marketing@envisionus.com** with questions or proposed changes. Propose changes through brand governance. Do not edit approved statements, names, or lockups without sign-off. The formal mission statement is board-adopted and changes only with board approval.

All lockup and naming-unit detail lives in Envision's **Standard Naming Units** document, referenced in [Section 3](docs/03-brand-architecture.md). Insert that content where marked.
