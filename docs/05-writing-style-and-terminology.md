# Section 5: Writing style and terminology

## Terminology (hard rule)

**Use.** _people who are blind or have low vision_, _individuals who are blind or have low vision_, _people with vision loss_, or _vision loss_. Always person-first.

**Never use.** _impaired_, _visually impaired_, _blind or visually impaired_, or _blind people_. The word _impaired_ is the core issue. Avoid it in every form. Do not use the acronym _BVI_, which expands to a banned phrase.

## Universal language rules

- Use person-first language always.
- Use _sighted_ or _typical vision_, never _normal vision_.
- Use _guide dog_, never _seeing eye dog_.
- Use sentence case always, except where brand standards specifically require otherwise, such as all-caps subheads in designed materials.
- Keep claims measurable where possible. Stats, outcomes, and proof points over vague assertions.
- Use active voice over passive. Short, direct sentences over long compound constructions.
- Avoid sight-based idioms in mission-facing content. Prefer _experience_, _understand_, _find_, _notice_ over _see_, _look_, _watch_, _blind to_, _in the dark_.
- Never use deficit language: _suffers_, _victim_, _afflicted_, _wheelchair-bound_, _confined to_.
- Never frame Envision's role as rescuing, saving, or giving back. Envision enables. People thrive.

## Prose mechanics: write plainly

Do not use decorative prose structures that simulate emotion or importance without adding meaning.

Avoid: vague interiority, anthropomorphized silence, not-this-but-that framing, triple-beat fragments, standalone _Because_ lines, repeated _And_ or _But_ openings, clichéd impact similes, and mechanical X-then-Y action pairs.

When tempted to use one of these, name what is happening directly, show the consequence, or cut the sentence.

Do not use AI-sounding phrasing: _delve_, _it's worth noting_, _in conclusion_, _I'd be happy to_, _certainly_, _absolutely_, _leverage_ outside a specific business context, _utilize_.

Write the word _and_, never a plus sign.

**Never use em dashes anywhere.**
