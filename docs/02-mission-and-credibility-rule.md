# Section 2: The Mission and Credibility Rule

This is the most important content decision in the Envision brand. Apply it before writing anything. It is the operating spine of this directive.

## The rule

**Mission follows credibility.** Establish that Envision can do the work first. Then mission becomes the reason to choose Envision over an equally capable competitor.

## Decision tree

- **Commercial or enterprise context?** Lead with capability, compliance, and proof points. Do not open with mission, nonprofit status, or charity framing. Mission may appear after credibility is established, never before. See [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md).
- **Government or federal context?** Lead with compliance and certifications. AbilityOne is a contract vehicle and qualification, not a charity credential. See [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md).
- **Buyer known to value nonprofit partnerships?** Mission may be introduced earlier. This is intel-driven, not a default. Confirm before applying. If unknown, default to capability-led.
- **Mission-program context** (Vision Rehabilitation, Mission Services, Child Development Center, Arts, Workforce, Research)? Lead with the person's experience and agency. Envision's role is enabling, never rescuing.
- **Donor or philanthropic context?** Lead with impact and stories. Quantify outcomes. Mission leads, but frame it through results, not need.
