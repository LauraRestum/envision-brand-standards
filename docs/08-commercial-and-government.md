# Section 8: Commercial and government go-to-market

Envision's commercial and government operations fund and extend the entire mission. They carry the most weight in revenue and the most risk in messaging, which is why they get their own section ahead of the mission pillars. The doctrine here is simple and absolute: **business first, mission throughout.**

## Doctrine

- **Capability opens.** Always lead with what Envision can do, not who Envision is.
- **U.S. manufacturing and compliance prove it.** Made-in-America credentials and federal compliance are primary content, not footnotes.
- **Mission runs throughout, and never opens.** Mission appears woven in as proof, for example _88 percent of direct labor performed by people who are blind or have low vision_, not as the opener.
- **Never open with nonprofit, charity, or mission framing** in commercial or government materials.

## Track 1: Commercial and enterprise

Private sector, any industry. The buyer is procurement, supply chain, or a category owner. They are not motivated by mission. They are motivated by reliability, quality, price, and risk reduction.

- **Lead with.** Capability, compliance certifications, proof points, on-time delivery, solution orientation, and enterprise track record.
- Position Envision as a partner who finds a way, not a vendor who fills an order.
- Name specifics wherever possible: delivery rates, capacity, turnaround, and named certifications.
- Do not conflate healthcare-specific language with all buyers. Envision serves any industry.

## Track 2: Government and federal

Federal, plus state and local. The buyer is a contracting officer, a contracting officer's representative, or a program office. The motion is different from enterprise: compliance and past performance carry the decision, and the language is procedural.

- **Lead with.** Compliance posture, certifications, contract vehicles, and documented past performance.
- Treat AbilityOne as a contract vehicle and a qualification, not a charity credential. See the AbilityOne guidance below.
- Frame set-asides as a procurement pathway the buyer can use, not a favor Envision is asking for.
- Keep compliance language exact. In contracting materials, precision is credibility.

## Federal channels and compliance vocabulary

Use these accurately in government materials. They signal that Envision speaks the buyer's language.

- **AbilityOne mandatory source.** Affiliated with National Industries for the Blind. SKILCRAFT-cataloged Envision products. 88 percent direct labor by people who are blind or have low vision, above the 75 percent threshold.
- **DLA Prime Vendor.** Active prime vendor contracts.
- **Base Supply Center network.** 16 sites across 10 states, part of the AbilityOne network spanning 160-plus bases. Pre-approved pricing, no competitive bid cycle. GPC and IMPAC cards accepted.
- **State Use programs.** Kansas State Use (Wichita) and Texas WorkQuest (Dallas).
- **NSN catalog.** Envision products carried under federal stock numbers.
- **SAM.gov.** Active, with campus-level CAGE and UEI registrations. Reference the registrations where relevant; keep the granular code list in the capabilities deck.
- **NAICS codes.** Identify Envision by the correct codes for the work.
- **Section 508.** Accessibility compliance for federal deliverables. A genuine Envision differentiator. Use it.

## Channel and gateway positioning

Federal Procurement Services is a managed pathway into federal and military procurement. Envision brings the channel infrastructure, compliance credentials, and procurement intelligence, so manufacturers can sell into government markets without building the federal machinery themselves. Partner products flow through the channels Envision already operates. Use this gateway framing for channel-control and OEM partnership conversations.

## AbilityOne positioning

AbilityOne is the contract mechanism, not a feel-good story. Frame it as a qualification and a pathway the buyer can use.

- AbilityOne is structurally central in the Base Supply Center and in many government contexts. It is not relevant to general private-sector commercial work, so do not introduce it there.
- Never frame AbilityOne as charity, and never pair it with giving-back language.

## When mission leads rather than supports

Mission runs throughout as proof, but it leads the opener only in two scenarios.

- **Scenario A.** An AbilityOne or Base Supply Center contract, where mission is structurally part of the vehicle.
- **Scenario B.** A buyer confirmed, through intel, to value nonprofit partnerships. Confirm before applying.

## Sample compliant openers

- Envision runs 410,000 square feet of U.S. manufacturing across two campuses, built for federal scale, with the compliance credentials federal buyers require already in place.
- As an AbilityOne mandatory source affiliated with National Industries for the Blind, Envision ships SKILCRAFT-cataloged product with federal qualification already done.
- Made in America: Berry, TAA, and Buy American compliant, ISO 9001:2015, pre-cleared across federal and state channels.

## Banned openers

- We're a nonprofit that employs people who are blind or have low vision.
- By choosing Envision, you give back and support a great cause.
- Envision is a mission-driven manufacturer.
- Help us support our workforce by placing your order with us.

## Sector notes

- **Healthcare.** Compliance and data handling lead. Do not let healthcare-specific language bleed into materials for other industries.
- **Defense and military.** Operational reliability over sentimentality. Route through the Base Supply Center pillar for installation retail. Shared values are earned, not performed.
- **Federal civilian.** Section 508, past performance, and contract vehicles lead. Procedural precision matters.
- **State and local.** Mirror the federal motion at smaller scale. Lead with capability and references, confirm the local vehicle and set-aside rules.

## Forbidden framing for this section

- No charity or nonprofit opener.
- No _we employ the blind_ or _we give jobs to_ language.
- No _giving back_.
- No _supporting our troops_ as a device.
- No _mission-driven manufacturing_.
- No _Envision Xpress_. Always _Envision Base Supply Center_.
