# Section 11: Asset type rules

Apply asset type rules on top of pillar, audience, and universal rules.

## Social media post

- Short, direct, human. Conversational tone. No jargon.
- Lead with the hook. The first line must earn the read.
- Person-first language always. No em dashes. No AI-sounding phrasing.
- Hashtags sparingly, only when they add reach.
- Images candid, never staged or inspirational-poster.

## Email

- Subject line specific and benefit-forward. No clickbait.
- Opening line gets to the point. No _I hope this email finds you well._
- Short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. One clear call to action.
- Signature: name, title, Envision, and contact.
- Tone adjusts to pillar and audience.

## Press release

- Standard AP style. Lead paragraph covers who, what, when, where, why in 40 words or fewer.
- One strong quote from a named Envision leader. Not generic.
- Mission context belongs in the boilerplate, not the lead.
- Always include the standard Envision boilerplate. Never use em dashes.

## Sales and capabilities deck

- Lead with capability, always. See [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md).
- Proof points before mission, always. Certifications, compliance, delivery metrics are primary.
- Mission is the closer, not the opener, if it appears at all.
- No charity framing. No nonprofit opener.

## Web copy

- Montserrat. Sentence case. Short, bold, outcome-focused headlines.
- Scannable body. Short paragraphs. Subheads every three to four paragraphs.
- Calls to action use action verbs: _Get started_, _Learn more_, _Contact us_. Not _Click here_.
- Accessible: alt text on all images. No sight-based idioms.

## Donor communication

- Lead with story. Specific person, specific outcome.
- Quantify impact: numbers, outcomes, proof points.
- Gratitude is genuine, not performative.
- Donor names used appropriately in this context.

## Internal communication

- Direct and clear. Less formal than external.
- Mission-connected tone. Staff should feel the purpose in their work.
- Consistent with brand voice, but warmer and more candid.
