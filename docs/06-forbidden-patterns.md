# Section 6: Forbidden patterns

These patterns must never appear in any Envision content regardless of context, pillar, or audience.

- Em dashes anywhere.
- The word _impaired_ in any form, including _visually impaired_ and _blind or visually impaired_.
- The acronym _BVI_.
- _The blind_ or _blind people_ as a noun. Always _people who are blind or have low vision_.
- _Normal vision_. Always _sighted_ or _typical vision_.
- _Despite vision loss_, or any despite-the-condition construction.
- _Suffers from_ in relation to vision loss or any condition.
- _Giving back_ in reference to Envision's commercial work.
- _Supporting our troops_ as a framing device.
- _We make people thrive_. People thrive. Envision enables.
- _We give jobs to the blind_. Objectifying.
- _Productive members of society_. Condescending.
- _Overcoming their limitations_. Deficit framing.
- _Seeing eye dog_. Always _guide dog_.
- _Wheelchair-bound_ or _confined to a wheelchair_.
- _Special needs_. Use specific, person-first language.
- _All abilities_ in the Child Development Center context.
- _Forward-thinking_. Use _future-ready_.
- _Optimize_. Use _improve_. _Utilize_. Use _use_. _Initiate_. Use _start_.
- _Innovative research_ or _cutting-edge_ without specific proof.
- _Envision Xpress_. Retired. Always _Envision Base Supply Center_.
- Donor names in general materials. Donor-facing contexts only.
- Decorative prose: not-this-but-that framing, triple-beat fragments, standalone _Because_ lines, anthropomorphized silence, clichéd impact similes.
- AI-sounding phrasing: _delve_, _it's worth noting_, _certainly_, _absolutely_, _I'd be happy to_, _in conclusion_.
- Inspirational-poster language without substance behind it.
