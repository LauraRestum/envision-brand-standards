# Section 3: Brand architecture

## Core rule: lead with Envision

Envision is the primary brand. Lead with Envision when describing who we are, what we do, our mission, our impact, or our role in the community. Use program, location, foundation, service, and event names to add specificity. They clarify how the mission shows up. They never replace Envision as the lead brand. When multiple programs, services, or audiences appear together, default to Envision.

## Lockup model (hybrid)

Four physical-location entities keep their named lockups. Everything else is described in plain language under the Envision master brand. Do not use program acronyms such as ECDC.

### Named lockups (the four physical locations)

- Envision Arts Center
- Envision Base Supply Center
- Envision Child Development Center
- Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center

Everything else, including the Research Institute, Workforce Innovation, the foundations, and all programs, is written as a plain-language descriptor of Envision. All lockup, logo, and naming-unit detail defers to Envision's Standard Naming Units document.

## Do

- Envision provides employment, rehabilitation, education, research and support services for people who are blind or have low vision.
- Envision expands access to inclusive early education through its Child Development Center.
- Envision provides personalized rehabilitation services through its Vision Rehabilitation Center.
- Envision connects artists who are blind or have low vision with creative opportunities through its Arts Center.
- Envision advances research, innovation and evidence-based solutions through its Research Institute.
- Envision is hiring for roles across its Base Supply Center network.
- The White Cane Day Walk supports Envision's Dallas Foundation and helps advance our mission for people who are blind or have low vision in North Texas.

## Avoid

- Using **Envision Dallas** as the name of the organization when the message is about Envision's broader mission.
- Leading with a program, location, foundation, or event name without connecting it back to Envision.
- Making programs, services, locations, or foundations feel disconnected from the enterprise brand, or treating their names as separate organizations.
- Using program acronyms such as ECDC.

## Naming governance

- Follow Envision's current Standard Naming Units document and approved logo lockup system for all naming, lockup, and naming-unit references.
- Do not create new program, service, foundation, campaign, location, or initiative names, or new logo lockups, unless approved through brand governance.
- Use an approved naming unit only when the context requires that level of specificity. Otherwise, lead with Envision and describe the program, service, foundation, location, or initiative in plain language.
- Keep each asset anchored to one primary context. Avoid stacking multiple Envision entity names in a single asset unless there is an approved reason, for example an event that formally benefits a specific foundation.

## Donor-recognized names

Use donor-recognized names only in donor-facing contexts: physical facility signage, donor materials, recognition assets, stewardship communications, and formal acknowledgments. Always write **Envision's Dallas Foundation** and **Envision's Wichita Foundation** with the apostrophe. Never _Envision Dallas Foundation_.

## Cross-market programs and services

Some programs and services operate across more than one market, or have local expressions not yet covered by the naming-unit system. When that happens, do not invent a new name or standalone brand. Lead with Envision, describe the program or service clearly, and add the local market only when it helps the audience understand where the work is happening.

**Pattern.** Envision + program or service description + local market.

**Do.** Envision's arts programming in North Texas. Envision's Child Development Center in the Dallas area. Envision's vision rehabilitation services in North Texas.

**Avoid inventing names such as.** Dallas Arts Center, Envision Dallas Arts Center, Dallas Child Development Center, Dallas Vision Rehabilitation Center.
