# Section 1: Identity

## Who Envision is

Envision is a mission-driven nonprofit organization with business-led operations. Founded in 1933, Envision expands access and opportunities for people who are blind or have low vision through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education and research.

## The integrated model

Envision pairs mission-driven programs and services with high-performing commercial operations that fund and extend them. This integrated model is one of Envision's greatest strengths and one of its most common communication challenges. Both sides serve the same purpose.

## The two voices

- **Commercial materials** should read like enterprise marketing: clear, capable, professional and solutions-focused.
- **Mission materials** should read with dignity, humanity and purpose.

Never blur the two voices, but treat both as serving the same purpose.

## The anchor idea: thriving

The single idea that anchors all Envision messaging is **thriving**, not merely surviving or managing. People who are blind or have low vision thrive with the right tools, support and opportunities at every stage of life. Let this idea shape tone and emphasis across both voices.

## The five mission pillars

Everything Envision does maps back to five pillars drawn from the mission. Every program and service connects to one or more.

- **Employment.** Meaningful career pathways, from job readiness and assistive technology training to competitive employment, so people who are blind or have low vision build independent careers.
- **Outreach.** Community-based programs, peer support and partnerships that extend Envision's reach beyond our walls and into everyday life.
- **Rehabilitation.** Clinical and adaptive skills training, from orientation and mobility to daily living, that helps people navigate the world on their own terms.
- **Education.** Strong foundations from the start: early childhood development, integrated classrooms, and continuing education that equips professionals who serve people who are blind or have low vision.
- **Research.** Advancing vision science through clinical trials, technology development and published research, from the lab to daily life.

## Core values

- **Integrity.** We do what's right, always.
- **Curiosity.** We ask questions, seek solutions, and never stop learning.
- **Passion.** We care deeply about our mission and the people we serve.
- **Initiative.** We take action and lead with purpose.
- **Teamwork.** We're stronger together.
- **Excellence.** We set the bar high and exceed expectations.

## Approved statements

Use these approved forms. When a short description is needed, default to the Elevator Pitch.

### Mission statement (formal, use verbatim)

> To improve the quality of life and provide inspiration and opportunity for people who are blind or have low vision through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education and research.

### Promise

> Envision supports people who are blind or have low vision, and their families, caregivers and communities, by creating pathways to independence, opportunity and long-term success.

### Elevator pitch (default short description)

> Envision expands access and opportunities for people who are blind or have low vision through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education and research. As one of the nation's largest employers of people with vision loss, Envision delivers an integrated system of programs, services and career pathways that build independence and support long-term success.
