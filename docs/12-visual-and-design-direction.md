# Section 12: Visual and design direction

This section governs how Envision looks: logo, color, typography, imagery, and accessibility. It is the visual companion to the writing rules and carries the same authority. Everything here follows the universal rules. Any embedded copy, caption, or alt text must pass [Section 5](05-writing-style-and-terminology.md) and [Section 6](06-forbidden-patterns.md), so a designed asset can fail brand review on its words alone.

Where visual identity concerns names, lockups, or specific logo files, defer to Envision's Standard Naming Units document and the brand asset library. This section sets direction. It does not replace those sources.

## One visual system, two voices

The two voices in [Section 1](01-identity.md#the-two-voices) share one visual system. The logo, palette, and typefaces do not change between them. The emphasis does.

- **Commercial materials.** Clean, capable, professional. Lead with primary blue and navy, generous white space, and capability-forward imagery. This is enterprise marketing.
- **Mission materials.** Warm, human, dignified. Use green as the accent that signals mission work, and candid, person-first imagery.

Never blur the two. Match the visual emphasis to the context you identified in the order of operations.

## Logo

- **Preferred mark.** The horizontal Envision master logo is the default for most applications. Use it unless space or design constraints require a variation.
- **Variations.** Horizontal, vertical, and icon-only (the hands mark) exist for cases where the preferred mark cannot be accommodated. The icon-only mark is reserved for app icons, social avatars, and favicons.
- **Clear space.** Always leave clear space around the logo equal to at least the height of the _E_ in Envision on all sides. This applies to print, digital, and signage.
- **Never alter the mark.** Do not rotate, stretch, recolor, add effects to, or rearrange the components of the logo. Do not place it over busy backgrounds or substitute the typeface. Use approved files as provided.
- **Backgrounds.** The icon-only mark is supplied white on transparent. Place it on a dark background, such as navy, so it stays visible. Do not recolor it to force contrast.

### Lockups and hierarchy

Logo hierarchy follows the brand architecture in [Section 3](03-brand-architecture.md).

- The four named physical locations keep their lockups: Envision Arts Center, Envision Base Supply Center, Envision Child Development Center, and Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center. These are location identifiers under the master brand, not independent logos.
- Everything else, including the Research Institute, Workforce Innovation, Mission Services, Continuing Education, and all programs, is referenced by name in text under the Envision master logo. Programs and services do not get their own logos.
- Foundation lockups are donor-facing only. Use them on facility signage, donor materials, and formal acknowledgments. Always write **Envision's Dallas Foundation** and **Envision's Wichita Foundation** with the apostrophe.
- Do not create new lockups. Defer all lockup and naming-unit detail to the Standard Naming Units document.

### Retired marks (never use)

- The old Envision Arts logo. Use Envision Arts Center under the master logo.
- The old Child Development Center circular logo. Use Envision Child Development Center under the master logo.
- Any previous independently operating sub-brand logos.
- Envision Xpress. Retired in name and mark. Always Envision Base Supply Center.

## Color

### Primary

| Color | Hex | Pantone | Role |
|-------|-----|---------|------|
| Blue | `#003087` | PMS 287C | Primary brand color. Anchors most applications. |
| Green | `#78BE21` | PMS 368C | Primary accent. Signals mission, energy, and highlights. |

Primary blue is `#003087` and navy is `#002855`. These are the source of truth. Do not substitute other blues found in older internal decks.

### Extended palette

Use the extended palette sparingly, for differentiation and emphasis. It supports the primaries. It does not replace them.

| Color | Hex | Pantone |

| Navy | `#002855` | PMS 295C |
| Bright Blue | `#41B6E6` | PMS 298C |
| Forest Green | `#00491E` | PMS 3537C |

- Lead with blue and navy. Use green as the accent that carries mission warmth.
- Do not introduce colors outside this system. When you need a warning or a negative accent, use terracotta rather than adding a new red.
- Keep color use purposeful. Bold and vibrant, never busy.

## Typography

Three typefaces, each with one role. Never substitute one for another.

| Typeface | Role | Use |
|----------|------|-----|
| Gotham | Print primary | Headlines and body copy in print and designed materials. Confident and clear. |
| Montserrat | Web and digital | Headlines and body copy in all digital and HTML formats. Available through Google Fonts. |
| Humanist | Logo only | Reserved for the logo. Not for everyday use. Reserving it for the logo strengthens the identity. |

- Never use Gotham on the web. It is the print primary and not web-safe. Digital work uses Montserrat.
- Set body copy and most headers in sentence case, per [Section 5](05-writing-style-and-terminology.md). All-caps is reserved for subheads and labels in designed materials.
- Keep the type scale consistent within an asset. Favor clear hierarchy over decorative variety.

## Imagery

- **Real people, real moments.** Use candid, natural photography that reflects the diversity of the people Envision serves. Avoid staged or generic stock imagery.
- **Tone.** Empowering and optimistic, focused on storytelling. Show people working, learning, creating, and connecting.
- **Dignity.** Never frame people who are blind or have low vision as passive, pitied, or defined by vision loss. Lead with the person and their agency, the same way the writing rules do.
- **Craft.** Use depth of field to draw attention to the subject. Show people in action rather than posed.
- **Words in images.** Every image needs alt text. Captions and embedded copy follow the same terminology and forbidden-pattern rules as body text. No sight-based idioms in mission-facing content.

## Accessibility

Accessible design is mission-critical for Envision, not an enhancement. Apply these standards to every asset.

- **Contrast.** Text and meaningful graphics meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA: a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text and 3:1 for large text. Verify any new color pairing against this threshold before use.
- **Approved high-contrast pairings.** These combinations meet AA and cover most needs:

  | Background | Text | 
  |------------|------|
  | Blue `#003087` | White |
  | Navy `#002855` | White |
  | White | Blue `#003087` |
  | White | Navy `#002855` |
  | Navy `#002855` | Green `#78BE21` |
  | White | Charcoal `#53565A` |
  | White | Forest Green `#00491E` |

- **Never rely on color alone** to carry meaning. Pair color with text, shape, or icon.
- **Alt text on all images.** Describe the content and function, not the decoration.
- **Legibility.** Size type for reading. Avoid light weights at small sizes, and keep generous line spacing.
- **Digital.** Use semantic structure, support keyboard navigation, provide sufficient tap-target size, and test with a screen reader before shipping.

## Do

- Use the approved logo, palette, and typefaces as provided.
- Maintain clear space around the logo.
- Match visual emphasis to the commercial or mission context.
- Design to WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and add alt text to every image.
- Choose candid, person-first imagery.

## Avoid

- Altering, recoloring, or rebuilding the logo, or reviving a retired mark.
- Introducing colors or typefaces outside this system, or setting web copy in Gotham.
- Staged, pitying, or deficit-framed imagery.
- Conveying meaning through color alone, or shipping images without alt text.
- Forbidden language in captions, labels, or alt text. See [Section 6](06-forbidden-patterns.md).

## Assets and governance

Logo files, color profiles, and templates live in the brand asset library. Lockup and naming detail defers to the Standard Naming Units document. Propose new visual assets, palette additions, or exceptions through brand governance. See [Section 16](16-governance.md).
