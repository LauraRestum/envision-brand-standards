# Section 9: How to write and talk about each part of Envision

Identify the part, then use the ready language below on top of the universal rules and the Mission and Credibility Rule. Lift the sample lines directly. Where a stat or proof point belongs, a slot is marked in brackets; fill it with verified content, never an estimate. Commercial and government work is governed by [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md).

## Envision (the whole organization)

- **What it is.** Envision is a mission-driven nonprofit organization with business-led operations, founded in 1933, that expands access and opportunities for people who are blind or have low vision through employment, outreach, rehabilitation, education and research.
- **Tone.** Confident, integrated, dual-voiced. One organization with two strengths.
- **Lead with.** Envision as the primary brand. Let programs add specificity, never replace it.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision expands access and opportunities for people who are blind or have low vision across Wichita and the Dallas area.
  - As one of the nation's largest employers of people with vision loss, Envision pairs commercial and government operations with mission programs that fund and extend each other.
  - Envision helps people build independence and long-term success through employment, education, rehabilitation, research and community programs.
- **Don't say.** Headquartered in Wichita. Envision Dallas as the organization's name. Charity, or the visually impaired. Leading with one program as if it were the whole organization.
- **One-liner.** Envision is a nonprofit social enterprise that expands access and opportunity for people who are blind or have low vision.
- **What thriving looks like.** People who are blind or have low vision building independent, full lives, funded by an organization whose commercial strength makes that work possible.

## Envision Arts Center

- **What it is.** Envision's studio, gallery and community creative space in Wichita, where artists who are blind or have low vision make and show work, expanding over time beyond visual art.
- **Tone.** Expressive, celebratory, community-rooted.
- **Lead with.** The work, the space, the community, the artist.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision's Arts Center connects artists who are blind or have low vision with studio space, exhibitions and a creative community.
  - Work from Envision's Arts Center [exhibition or show name] opens [date] in Wichita.
  - At Envision's Arts Center, artists who are blind or have low vision show, sell and grow their practice.
- **Don't say.** Art therapy. Creative outlet for coping. Helps them deal with vision loss. Despite their disability. Anything clinical or rehabilitative.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Arts Center is a studio, gallery and creative community for artists who are blind or have low vision.
- **What thriving looks like.** An artist whose work is seen, valued and sold, inside a real creative community.

## Envision Base Supply Center

- **What it is.** Envision's retail operation on military installations, built on the AbilityOne contract, serving the military community with reliable, consistent supply.
- **Tone.** Direct, earned, no-nonsense. Respect over sentimentality.
- **Lead with.** Operational reliability, product quality, consistent supply, shared values.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision Base Supply Center keeps installation shelves stocked with the products the military community relies on, on time and in full.
  - As a qualified AbilityOne provider, Envision Base Supply Center delivers consistent retail supply across its network.
  - Envision Base Supply Center is hiring across its locations.
- **Don't say.** Giving back. Supporting our troops. Helping the blind serve the brave. Envision Xpress. Any charity framing.
- **One-liner.** Envision Base Supply Center is Envision's AbilityOne retail operation serving military installations.
- **What thriving looks like.** A service member who gets what they need without friction, and a workforce that found purpose and income.

## Envision Child Development Center

- **What it is.** Envision's early childhood education program, in Wichita and the Dallas area, where children who are blind or have low vision learn in an integrated classroom alongside sighted peers.
- **Tone.** Warm, parent-facing, outcome-grounded.
- **Lead with.** The integration model, facility quality, future-ready curriculum, community roots.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision's Child Development Center brings together children who are blind or have low vision and sighted peers in one future-ready classroom.
  - Families in the Dallas area choose Envision's Child Development Center for its integration model and [quality or accreditation marker].
  - At Envision's Child Development Center, early learning is built on access from the start.
- **Don't say.** All abilities. Children with differences. Forward-thinking. Special needs. Inclusive as a label without showing it. Clinical or deficit language.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Child Development Center is an integrated early childhood program for children who are blind or have low vision and their sighted peers.
- **What thriving looks like.** A child who enters kindergarten ready, and a family that found a community that understood from the start.

## Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center

- **What it is.** Envision's clinical vision rehabilitation service, in Wichita and the Dallas area, offering low vision evaluations, assistive technology training and independent living skills. Often a person's first contact with Envision.
- **Tone.** Warm with clinical credibility. Hope-forward but grounded.
- **Lead with.** What's possible: the tools, training and expertise, and the client's agency.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision's Vision Rehabilitation Center helps people who are blind or have low vision build the skills and tools for the routines they want.
  - Newly diagnosed clients work with Envision's Vision Rehabilitation Center on assistive technology, daily living skills and what comes next.
  - Physicians refer to Envision's Vision Rehabilitation Center for low vision evaluation and rehabilitation.
- **Don't say.** Despite vision loss. We helped her get her life back. Gave him hope. The blind patient. Pity or charity framing.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Vision Rehabilitation Center provides low vision evaluation, assistive technology training and independent living skills.
- **What thriving looks like.** A person who moves through the world with confidence, equipped with the right tools and skills.

## Research Institute

- **What it is.** Envision's research arm, advancing vision science and applied innovation through labs, fellowships and university and industry partnerships.
- **Tone.** Credible, precise, forward-looking. Academic but accessible.
- **Lead with.** Specific research, named partnerships, measurable outcomes.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision's Research Institute studies [named study or focus area] in partnership with [named partner].
  - Through its Research Institute, Envision advances evidence-based solutions for [specific outcome].
  - Envision's Research Institute fellows are investigating [specific topic].
- **Don't say.** Innovative research. Cutting-edge. Groundbreaking. We're committed to finding solutions. Anything vague without specific proof.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Research Institute advances vision science and applied innovation through partnerships and fellowships.
- **What thriving looks like.** A breakthrough that changes how vision loss is understood or treated, and a fellow who goes on to lead the field.

## Workforce Innovation (including Level-Up)

- **What it is.** Envision's career development and employment pathways, including the youth Level-Up program (national residential Pre-ETS, ages 14 to 22, at Wichita State University) and adult programs such as Pathways and the Workforce Readiness Bootcamp.
- **Tone.** Motivating, practical, coaching not cheerleading.
- **Lead with.** Outcomes, program specifics, real skills, real jobs.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Through Workforce Innovation, Envision turns training into real jobs for people who are blind or have low vision.
  - Level-Up is Envision's residential Pre-ETS program for students ages 14 to 22, aligned to the Expanded Core Curriculum.
  - Envision's adult workforce programs build assistive technology and job-readiness skills that lead to placement.
- **Don't say.** Despite their disability they found work. We help the blind find jobs. Productive members of society. Overcoming limitations. Do not let Level-Up dominate. College Success Program and Talent Network are retired.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Workforce Innovation builds skills and pathways that lead to real employment for people who are blind or have low vision.
- **What thriving looks like.** A young person with direction and skills, and an adult who lands a job and keeps it.
- **Note.** For Level-Up, the primary marketing audience is TVIs and VR counselors, not the youth directly.

## Mission Services

- **What it is.** Envision's community-based programs supporting people who are blind or have low vision across every stage of life. Lead with the continuum, not a program list.
- **Tone.** Compassionate, dignified, never pitying.
- **Lead with.** The continuum, the community, the person's experience and choices.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Through its community programs, Envision supports people who are blind or have low vision at every stage of life.
  - Envision's community programs meet people where they are, on their own terms.
  - People connect, participate and thrive through Envision's community programs.
- **Don't say.** We take care of. The less fortunate. Those who cannot care for themselves. We give them. Suffering from vision loss. Do not enumerate every program.
- **One-liner.** Envision's community programs support people who are blind or have low vision across every stage of life.
- **What thriving looks like.** A person with community and support that fits their life at every stage, and a family that is not navigating it alone.

## Continuing Education

- **What it is.** Envision's professional education for vision rehabilitation and allied health practitioners, including Grand Rounds, the Envision Conference and on-demand courses. Internally Envision University; externally always Continuing Education.
- **Tone.** Authoritative, collegial, peer-to-peer.
- **Lead with.** Content quality, faculty, networking, CEU credits.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision's Continuing Education offers CEU-eligible courses, Grand Rounds and the Envision Conference for vision rehabilitation professionals.
  - Practitioners earn [CEU count] CEUs through Envision's Continuing Education.
  - The Envision Conference convenes the field around [topic or faculty].
- **Don't say.** Learn from the experts. Accessible education for all. We make learning easy. Envision University in external materials.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Continuing Education delivers CEU-eligible professional education for vision rehabilitation and allied health practitioners.
- **What thriving looks like.** A clinician who leaves with tools they use Monday morning, and a field that is better connected and equipped.

## Commercial and government

- **What it is.** Envision's commercial and government operations: U.S. manufacturing across two campuses (polymer and film, textile and apparel, reflective safety systems, metal fabrication, print, bindery and writing instruments, fulfillment, kitting and assembly) plus services (contact center, secure document services, design and sourcing, accessibility services, federal procurement, quality assurance). 410,000 square feet built for federal scale. These fund and extend the mission.
- **Tone.** Business-first, capability-led, credibility-forward.
- **Lead with.** Capability, U.S. domestic manufacturing, compliance, proof points. Business first, mission throughout. Governed by [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md).
- **Say it like this.**
  - Envision runs 410,000 square feet of U.S. manufacturing across two campuses, built for federal scale.
  - As an AbilityOne mandatory source affiliated with National Industries for the Blind, Envision delivers SKILCRAFT-cataloged product with federal qualification already done.
  - Made in America and compliance-ready: Berry, TAA and Buy American compliant, ISO 9001:2015, with 88 percent of direct labor performed by people who are blind or have low vision.
- **Don't say.** We're a nonprofit. We employ the blind. Giving back. Mission-driven manufacturing. Any charity or mission opener.
- **One-liner.** Envision provides U.S.-based manufacturing, fulfillment, contact center and federal procurement services for commercial and government buyers, built for federal scale.
- **What thriving looks like.** A workforce producing enterprise-grade output, and clients who get what they need on time and at spec.
- **Note.** See [Section 8](08-commercial-and-government.md) for the full two-track playbook, proof architecture, federal channels and openers.

## The foundations (Dallas and Wichita)

- **What it is.** Envision's Dallas Foundation and Envision's Wichita Foundation are the donor-facing entities that raise and steward philanthropic support for Envision's mission. The names always carry the apostrophe, and they appear only in donor-facing contexts.
- **Tone.** Grateful, impact-driven, relationship-focused.
- **Lead with.** Impact and stories, backed by outcomes. Mission leads here, framed through results.
- **Say it like this.**
  - Gifts to Envision's Dallas Foundation expand opportunity for people who are blind or have low vision across North Texas.
  - Envision's Wichita Foundation stewards donor support that funds [program or outcome].
  - The White Cane Day Walk supports Envision's Dallas Foundation and advances Envision's mission in North Texas.
- **Don't say.** Envision Dallas Foundation without the apostrophe. Foundation names in general, non-donor materials. The less fortunate or any pity framing.
- **One-liner.** Envision's Dallas Foundation and Envision's Wichita Foundation raise and steward philanthropic support for Envision's mission.
- **What thriving looks like.** Donors who see their investment create something real, and a relationship that deepens over time.
- **Note.** Donor-facing contexts only.
